Birla Mandir

Birla Mandir is one of the major and famous religious tourist places in India. It is situated in the capital city of Rajasthan, Jaipur. This temple is built in 1998 around 20th century by Birla family and it is devoted to Lakshmi and lord Vishnu. This temple is situated at the mountains of Moti Dungari in the city of Jaipur.

Brahma Temple

Brahma Mandir or Brahma Temple is 2000 years old temple. It is situated near to Pushkar Lake. This temple is devoted to Lord Brahma and some statues of Lord Saraswathi are also seen here. It is believed that Lord Brahma came to earth and did a yajna at the same spot where this temple is built now.

Karni Mata Temple

Karni Mata Temple is situated in Bikaner and this is exclusively situated to Goddess Mata Karni who is considered to be an epitome of Goddess Durga. The main ritual of this temple is to feed the rats. It has more than 20000 black rats in this temple. So it is famously known as rat temple.

Mehandipur Balaji Temple

Mehandipur Balaji Mandir is a noted Hindu temple in Dausa district of Rajasthan, dedicated to one of the Hindu God Hanuman. This temple is placed in the Kaurali district to worship the Lord Hanuman.

Ambika Mata temple

Ambika Mata temple can be seen near Udaipur about 50km away. It is situated in the village, Jagat. This temple mainly worships Goddess Durga. It is renowned for its exquisite carvings which lend it a resemblance to the famous Khajuraho temples in Madhya Pradesh.

Khatu Shyam Ji Mandir

Khatu Shyam Ji Mandir is located in the district of Sikar, Rajasthan, and is considered as one of the most important pilgrim destinations in the state. According to Hindu mythology, Khatu Shyam Ji is the manifestation of the son of Ghatotkacha, Barbarika.

Paragliding

Paragliding under the sparkling skies of Rajasthan will be a very special moment for anyone. The lakes, hills, and the ancient monuments will just look mesmerizing from a great height which makes paragliding, one of the most popular adventure sports of Rajasthan.

Water Sports

There is no dearth of water bodies or lakes in the state of Rajasthan. At present, the activities near lakes are increasing with a drastic number. You can actively participate in these adventurous sports in Rajasthan if you so wish. You can try parasailing or boating on the lakes.

Wildlife Safari

On the other side, the camel safari will provide a glimpse of the rustic lifestyle of the desert people. You can also taste Indian food, particularly of Rajasthan during this safari. If you are really lucky then you can come across various types of wild animals who move around the forest.

Trekking

Trekking is one of the most preferred adventure sports in Rajasthan. Trekking in Rajasthan is much thrilling, especially in the Aravalli range. The majority of tourists mainly come to Rajasthan for this purpose. Aravalli is one of the oldest ranges that offer excellent scope for trekking.

Jeep Safari

A jeep safari is one of the most noted adventure sports in Rajasthan that must be tried by everyone. A simple round in a jeep along the desert will give you a fabulous experience. Wildlife

The plethora of wildlife sanctuaries in Rajasthan has made it an oasis to innumerable species of rare birds and mammals. From semi green forests present in Mount Abu to the beautiful dry grasslands of the desert, from wetlands of Bharatpur to deciduous forest of Aravali, the varied topography attracts many migratory birds.

Sariska National Park, Alwar

The Sariska Tiger Reserve is situated in the Alwar district in the state of Rajasthan and is one of the important national parks in Rajasthan. It was the hunting grounds of the State of Alwar. It also has a significant number of bird species such as the Great Indian Horned Owl, crested serpent eagle, golden backed woodpecker, tree pie, sand grouse, bush quail, grey partridge, peafowl, and more.

Desert National Park, Jaisalmer

The Desert National Park is one of the important national parks in Rajasthan and is situated near the town of Jaisalmer. Sprawling over an area of 3162 square kilometres, the national park is one of the largest national parks in the country. The most important attraction of the national park is the Great Indian Bustard, which is a magnificent bird found in very few numbers.

Rajaji national Park

Close to the cities, yet offering a wild serenity that is exhilarating. That's the charm of the Rajaji National national park, most famous for its elephant population. Located at the Himalayan foothills, near Haridwar, Rishikesh and Dehradun, with Ganga and Song rivers, flowing through it, the park is known for its scenic beauty and rich bio-diversity.

Kumbhalgarh Wildlife Sanctuary, Kumbhalgarh

The Kumbhalgarh Wildlife Sanctuary is situated in the Rajsamand district of Rajasthan. The sanctuary is one of the most famous wildlife sanctuaries in Rajasthan. Visitors can enjoy foot tracking and horse safaris in the sanctuary, arranged by local tour operators. The route for safaris starts from the Kumbhalgarh Fort and continues till Ghanerao.

Sajjangarh Wildlife Sanctuary

The Sajjangarh Wildlife Sanctuary is situated at a distance of 5 km from the Sajjangarh Palace and surrounds the palace in Udaipur. The backdrop of Bansdara Hill enchants one with beautiful views of sunset and sunrise. The sanctuary houses sambars, chitals, wild boars, hyenas, blue bulls, panthers, hares, jackals etc, apart from that, you can also spot a variety of reptiles like crocodiles and alligators and birds.
