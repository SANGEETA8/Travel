Spritual Places

The whole state of Gujarat is highly opulent with godly places of pilgrimage and holy temples Ambaji, Somnath, Dwarka, Palitana, Girnar, and Dakor are the chief holy places of Gujarat.


Previous Next

Dwarkadhish Temple

Dwarka on the western tip of the Kathiawar Peninsula is clubbed with the holiest sites in India – the Char Dhams that include Badrinath, Puri and Rameshwaram. It is believed that Lord Krishna arrived here from Braj in Uttar Pradesh to build the city. The temple was established by his grandson.

Rukmini Devi Temple

Located relatively on the outskirts, the Rukmini Mata Temple commemorates Lord Krishna’s queen. The temple is said to be more than 2500 years old but it may have been reconstructed over time. The present temple is said to belong to the 12th century. It is much more modest than Dwarkadhish in structure and sculptures but inspires the same devotional fervour.

Shamlaji Temple

Sakshi Gopal or Gadadhar is a black representation of Vishnu worshipped at the Shamlaji Temple. This is one of the rare temples of Lord Krishna in which cow idols are also worshipped depicting his childhood as a cowherd. For the Vaishnavites Shamlaji is one of the hundred and fifty four most important places of pilgrimage in India.

Shri Ranchhodraiji Maharaj Temple, Dakor

The main temple, enclosed by a fort wall, is situated near the bank of the holy lake Gomti in the midst of the main bazaar of Dakor. The temple is covered with 8 domes and 24 turrets, with the central dome reaching a height of 27 m.

Bhalka Teerth

The temple commemorates the episode when a poacher, Jara, mistakenly hit the Lord with his arrow, thinking it’s a deer. The Lord left for his heavenly abode after this episode, but not before walking 4km from here to Shri Goloknathdham Tirth. In the 9th century AD Shri Vallabhacharya gave discourses on Shrimad Bhagavat Gita here for 9 full days.

Somnath Jytorilinga Temple

The Somnath Jyotirlinga Temple is first of the twelve Jyotirlinga shrines of Lord Shiva. It is located on the western coast of Gujarat and is one of the oldest temples of the country. It has been mentioned in ancient texts like Shreemad Bhagavat Gita, Skandpuran, Shivpuran and Rig-Veda which signifies the importance of this temple as one of the most celebrated pilgrimage sites.

Adventure

The various adventure sports in Gujarat have always kept travelers hooked to the state. The curated list has some of the best adventure sports to take the plunge into while in Gujarat are Jungle Trekking, River Rafting, Paragliding, Zorbing, SkyDiving etc.

Jungle Trekking

One of the famed sports in the adventure category is trekking. Gujarat is bordered by many hill ranges and thus has ample trekking options in its bounty. The hill ranges of Saputara, Girnar, Sahyadri, and polo forests are the best places to go trekking in the state.

River Rafting

River rafting is yet another of the exciting options in the state for all the adventurists out there. The River Narmada which flows through the mainland of Gujarat is an ideal river for rafting in the state. While the rapids increase the thrill of the journey by 10 folds, this daunting sport can be accomplished in Vadodara and Ahmedabad across the state.

Paragliding

Bestowed with hill ranges and towering peaks, Gujarat has become an epicenter for Paragliding activities. There are a few places in the state like Saputara, Pavangarh, and Junagarh where one can enjoy this challenging activity along with soaking in the natural beauty of the locales.

Zorbing

Body zorbing is another recreational activity that has been embraced by people of the state. The energizing sport is carried out by a person wearing a zorb ball of about 4 feet in diameter, and then tumbling on the ground with the help of the enlarged bubble.

SkyDiving

All the people opting for the sport get a chance to dive from a height of over 13,000 feet including free fall for a minute before the gradual descent comes into play. With enriching landscapes and surreal views of the city, this opportunity should not be pushed away.

Rock Climbing

Located at a distance of 100 m from the city, the hills scattered around the village are a stern challenge for every adventure junkie. Home to voluminous boulders made of granite, these structures stand across the hilly terrains and make up for a humongous task.

Wildlife

The state is also one of the wealthiest wildlife destinations in the country that boasts of rich biodiversity. With its diverse natural habitats ranging from grasslands to lush forests and from sea coasts to lakes, Gujarat attracts tourists from all over the world.

Gir National Park

Gir National Park in Gujarat is the only place in India having the largest population of Asiatic lions. Apart from lions, the park is home to wild boar, sambar deer, spotted deer, Nilgai, and carnivorous animals, including hyena, wild cat, fox and leopard, and more than 300 species of birds.

Velavadar National Park

Velavadar National Park is famous for having the rare species of blackbuck. The Velavadar blackbuck national park also has mammals like nilgai (another Indian antelope), jungle cat, jackal, fox, wolf, and more. It is a perfect place to weave some unforgettable memories with your friends and family.

Marine National Park

In 1980, the area was known as a marine sanctuary, and later it was declared a national park in 1982. Marine National Park has three distinct divisions like the coastal sea, the mangrove backwaters, and the salty marshland. Here, one can spot a number of marine animals like sea anemones, stingrays, octopuses, pufferfishes, sea stars, crabs, and more.

Jambughoda Wildlife Sanctuary

The Jambughoda Wildlife Sanctuary is known to be thronged by families, youngsters, and nature and wildlife lovers. Situated 70 km from Vadodara, the Jambughoda Wildlife Sanctuary was under the realm of Jambughoda’s princely state of Gujarat before Independence.

Barda Wildlife Sanctuary

Located at a distance of 15 kilometres from Porbandar, the Barda Wildlife Sanctuary is made up of lush green forests, surrounded by dams, narrow streams, and rivers. Also known as Rana Barda and Jam Barda, this sanctuary provides a home to many endangered species of birds, mammals, and reptiles.

Vansda National Park

The main attractions of this national park in Gujarat include 450 species of plants and around 115 species of birds. The park is home to grey hornbill, paradise flycatcher, racket-tailed drongo, leaf birds, yellow back sunbird, peafowls, jungle babbler, and globally threatened forest spotted owlet. Here you can also see mongooses, barking deer, jungle cat, leopard, and hyena.
