KARNATAKA

Karnataka is a state in southwest India with Arabian Sea coastlines. The capital, Bengaluru (formerly Bangalore), is a high-tech hub known for its shopping and nightlife. To the southwest, Mysore is home to lavish temples including Mysore Palace, former seat of the region’s maharajas. Spritual Places

Karnataka’s coastline spread across the districts of Uttara Kannada, Udupi and Dakshina Kannada, famed for its pristine beaches, also hosts numerous pilgrimage destinations. These scenic pilgrimage spots aren’t just spiritually satisfying, but gives travelers an opportunity to learn about the underlying cultural context of these regions.

Mahabaleshwar Temple, Gokarna

Gokarna is a coastal town that draws Hindu pilgrims, Sanskrit scholars, and beach buffs alike. Apart from its famed beaches and the Centre for Sanskrit Learning, it is home to many ancient temples. Gokarna is home to the legendary temple of Mahabaleshwara which houses the atma linga of lord Shiva.

Murudeshwara

Murudeshwara, located on the main Mangalore- Karwar Highway, is sandwiched between the picturesque Western Ghats and the Arabian Sea. Its main attraction is the Shiva temple next to the beach, built in the Dravidian style of architecture along with a towering statue of Shiva.

Mangala Devi Temple, Mangaluru

Mangaluru has the ancient 10th century Mangala Devi temple dedicated to Goddess Shakti in the form of Mangaladevi. The presiding deity, Mangaladevi, in the central shrine is in a seated posture. The Dasara celebrations here attract lots of devotees.

Sri Krishna Temple, Udupi

Thousands of devotees journey to this temple town to offer prayers to lord Krishna. The Krishna idol here can only be viewed through a 9 holed window called the Navagraha Kitiki. There is a beautiful water tank nearby called Madhwa Sarovara with a little mantapa in the centre. The image of lord Krishna is taken on a float during festivals.

Karkala

The towering 42 feet monolith of lord Gomateshwara, (the biggest one after the monolith at Shravanabelagola) standing atop a granite outcrop on the outskirts of the town, is the main attraction here. There is also a 700 year old stone temple dedicated to Lord Ananthapadmanabha

Durga Parameshwari Temple, Kateel

Located on an island in the Nandini river, this temple is situated east of Mangaluru in Kateel . The temple dedicated to Goddess Durga Parameshwari is in the form of an Udhbhava Moorthi (naturally formed). The pillars of the temple also have beautiful sculptures.

Adventure

Karnataka is one of the beautiful states in India that is blessed with natural and man-made attractions. From Bengaluru to Mysuru, from Coorg to Hampi, there are so many things to witness in the various places in the state.


Previous Next

Dandeli

Located on the banks of River Kali, Dandeli has been a favorite place for adventure freaks for a long time now. It is one of the water rafting spots in the state. Even if you do not know how to swim, you can take the chance of river rafting here as you will be accompanied by a guide.

Murudeshwara

If you mention Murudeshwara to your friends, most of them will tell you about the Shiva Temple. Well, this place has caught the attention of adventure lovers for offering various water sports. Some of the exciting things to do here are kayaking, parasailing, snorkeling, jet-skiing, and scuba diving. Specifically for scuba diving, you have to visit Netrani Island.

Ramanagara

Ramanagara is one of the top attractions in Karnataka. The amazing place is located at a distance of 50 km from Bengaluru. Ramanagara is known for its rocky hills where an interesting activity of rock climbing is conducted.

Malpe Beach

Malpe is a natural port located in the Udupi district of the state. The Malpe Beach is another preferred destination for adventure lovers. Some of the adrenaline-rushing activities that you can try here include jet-skiing, camel ride, parasailing, and boating. One of the nearby places to visit from here is the St. Mary’s Islands, which is also called Coconut Island.

Kumara Parvatha

Kumara Parvatha is one of the famous trekking places in Karnataka that is often visited by tourists who love trekking. It is the highest peak in the Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary, and also known as one of the most difficult trekking trails.

Coorg

Coorg is one of the places in Karnataka that has always been praised for its natural beauty and landscape. The beauty of the Western Ghats here along with the Coffee estates and perfect weather is mesmerizing. Apart from relaxing here, you can also try some adventure activities such as trekking, river rafting, and camping.

Wildlife

Karnataka is renowned for being one of India’s richest states when it comes to wildlife. A wildlife tour of Karnataka can be one of the most rewarding experiences. With a forest area of 38,720 sq km, more than 20% of the state of Karnataka is under forests.

Bandipur National Park

Bandipur is known especially for its large numbers of the Bengal tiger. It is known to offer more frequent tiger sightings than the other wildlife sanctuaries in the country. Bandipur is considered as one of the better-managed national parks in India. Apart from the tiger, the park is also home to other animals like gaur (a type of bull), sambhar, chital, mouse deer, sloth bear, and panther.

Bannerghatta National Park

Bannerghatta has several sections like the zoo and the butterfly park and offers separate safaris for herbivores like spotted deer and carnivores like lions and tigers. The park is currently home to 32 lions and 36 tigers. It is also renowned as the first biological park in India to have a separate fenced elephant sanctuary.

Dandeli wildlife sanctuary

Jungle jeep safaris in Dandeli wildlife sanctuary can be the most thrilling experience as many of the animals also climb atop the vehicles during these safaris. The Dandeli wildlife sanctuary is the second largest sanctuary in Karnataka. The nearby Sintheri rocks is a famous tourist attraction.

Kudremukh National Park

The name Kudremukha literally means “horse-face” which is how one side of the picturesque view from the mountain is shaped. Kudremukh has a very dense forest cover making it a great natural habitat for wildlife. Spotting animals here is not very easy and one needs to go deep into the jungle for a safari.

Bhadra wildlife sanctuary

It is an excellent biodiversity hotspot as it is home to dry and moist deciduous forests as well as semi evergreen ones. There are close to 33 tigers in Bhadra. Several other animals like elephant, gaur, sloth bear, jackal, wild dog, sambar, spotted deer, barking deer, mouse deer, wild boar, black leopard, jungle cat, common langur, and bonnet macaque.

Daroji Sloth Bear sanctuary

Located in the Ballari district in Karnataka, the Daroji sloth Bear sanctuary, as the name suggests, was created exclusively to preserve the Indian sloth bear. It is just 15 km away from the World Heritage site of Hampi.
